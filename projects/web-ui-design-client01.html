Freelance Client Project

Website UI Redesign

01. Introduction

A colleague of mine based in Canada approached me for a UI design role for his clients' website redesign. The goal was to establish an improved updated, modern look and feel for the website inline with their updated vision, target market and growing opportunities inside the company. Alongside improving its usability and optimize content presentation.


Task Given: To prepare samples of low fidelity wireframes for the home page redesign as this will set the overall structure of the entire website.

02. Research and Inspiration

Before beginning the design process, I conducted thorough research to understand user expectations and industry standards within the local construction and development sector. I looked into websites of business partners and specific competitors to identify common design patterns and opportunities for differentiation.

To establish a strong visual direction for me and my colleague, I curated references from Pinterest focusing on layout, color harmony, and imagery style. Additionally, the client provided a reference website, which helped ensure our design direction were well aligned.

03. Sketches to Wireframes

With the research insights in mind, I began sketching low-fidelity wireframes to restructure the site’s layout and create a more intuitive navigation experience. The primary focus was on enhancing readability, ensuring content clarity and establishing a smooth user-friendly flow.

After aligning with the client on the initial concepts, I developed high-fidelity wireframes in Figma, incorporating the brand’s color palette, typography, and imagery inspired by our visual references. The refined design introduced a simplified information hierarchy, clear navigation and prominent call-to-action areas.

04. Constraints and the Challenges

One of the main challenges arose from the client’s preference for a content management system (CMS) that would allow them to easily update content and publish blogs independently. To meet this requirement, my colleague recommended using WordPress as the CMS paired with the DIVI Theme Builder, a setup that was new to me.

Instead of viewing this as a limitation, I approached it as an exciting opportunity to learn and adapt. I spent time exploring both tools, experimenting with layout customization in DIVI, and testing how closely I could translate my Figma-approved wireframes within WordPress’s framework.

Through this process, I learned to customize DIVI beyond standard modules and implement my design system within the platform. In the end, I was able to faithfully recreate the approved design while ensuring the client retained full control over their content.

05. Testing and Implementation

During the development phase, my colleague and I worked closely to implement the final design using WordPress with the DIVI Theme Builder, ensuring strong alignment with the approved Figma prototypes. His guidance was instrumental, particularly as he introduced me to several new tools such as XAMPP for local testing and GitHub for version control, which significantly enhanced my technical workflow and understanding of the development process.

Together, we used XAMPP to test the site locally, assessing responsiveness, browser compatibility, and overall performance before deployment. The testing phase was highly collaborative: we reviewed user navigation and flow while I refined typography, spacing, and accessibility, and incorporated client feedback across multiple iterations. This teamwork ensured that the final build met both functional and aesthetic expectations.

Version control was managed through GitHub, allowing us to maintain clear documentation, track revisions efficiently, and collaborate seamlessly throughout development. My colleague’s ongoing support and technical insights were invaluable in creating a smooth, organized and well-executed development process.
